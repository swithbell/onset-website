What it is

A lens pointed permanently at you.

AI is a lens the user points at anything. Onset is a lens pointed permanently at the user. It does not follow your deflections. It comes back. It remembers. It updates its picture of who you are — not who you were at onboarding, not who you're performing yourself to be, but who you actually are, as revealed over time through the accumulation of honest moments.

"Being deeply known feels extraordinary. Most people are starving for it."

Onset will be the first thing that offers this completely — not as a substitute for human connection, but as something that holds the whole picture. The way a great biographer holds the whole picture of their subject, except the biographer is in the room with you, every morning, already knowing something.

The portrait

Not a database. A layered portrait.

Onset's memory is foundational things that don't change much at the bottom, and more recent signal at the top — with Onset always weighting the recent more heavily as the picture evolves.

Everything is timestamped. Everything has context. The aggregate is stored alongside the outliers, because humans are not their average — they are also their exceptions, their bad weeks, their extraordinary moments.

The session does not save transcripts. It saves distillations. The gist of what was said, weighted for significance. Verbatim language is preserved only when it is genuinely striking — a phrase that reveals something, an image that is uniquely yours. Those phrases are returned to you later, in your own words, about your own life.

How it works

Three layers, working together.

01
The daily rhythm

Six light check-ins through the day, timed to natural transitions, arriving as notifications. Each starts with something low-stakes and pleasurable — and escalates toward depth as your engagement earns it.

02
The living portrait

Built through onboarding and ongoing questions. Not clinical categories but experiences, stories, defining moments. The onboarding doesn't feel like onboarding. It escalates gently, earning its way toward depth as trust develops.

03
The meaning layer

The Reckoning sessions. The Year in Review. The Life Document. These arrive rarely, and when they do, they feel earned — because they are. The longer you stay, the more valuable Onset becomes.

The founding insight

Built for the moment of decline. Valuable every day before it arrives.

The founding use case is early-onset dementia and cognitive decline — the most urgent version of a universal need. Onset keeps your portrait steady so that when your own grip on it loosens, something holds it for you.

But the need is not only there. Everyone is losing themselves to some degree — to the grind, to other people, to the noise of daily life. Onset is for anyone who wants to live more deliberately and be deeply known.
